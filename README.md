## Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard (Excel)

### Business Problem
The objective of this project was to analyze coffee shop sales data to understand customer purchasing behavior, peak sales periods, product performance, and store-level trends in order to optimize operations and increase revenue.

### Approach
- Cleaned and structured transactional sales data  
- Used Excel (Pivot Tables, charts, and slicers) to aggregate and analyze data  
- Built an interactive dashboard to monitor KPIs such as total sales, footfall, average bill, and order behavior  
- Enabled filtering by month and day for dynamic analysis  

### Key Insights
- Peak sales occur during morning hours (8 AM – 10 AM), indicating strong demand during early hours  
- Coffee products contribute the largest share of total sales (~39%), making them the primary revenue driver  
- Barista Espresso is the top-performing product (~$91K in sales)  
- Larger-sized orders generate the highest revenue (~$237K), indicating customer preference for higher-value purchases  
- Sales remain relatively stable across weekdays, suggesting consistent customer flow  
- Certain store locations show higher footfall and revenue, highlighting location-based performance differences  

### Impact
This analysis can help businesses:
- Optimize staffing during peak hours  
- Focus on high-performing products for promotions  
- Improve inventory planning based on demand patterns  
- Enhance store-level strategy and performance tracking  

### Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables, Charts, Slicers, Dashboard Design)  

### Features
- KPI tracking (Sales, Footfall, Avg Bill, Orders)  
- Time-based analysis (Hourly, Monthly, Weekly)  
- Product and category performance breakdown  
- Interactive filters for dynamic exploration
